Problem Statement: Delay Avoidance

People need a better way to accurately track how long it will take them to get to class. This includes expected stoplight and crosswalk traffic.

Affinity Diagram: Delay Avoidance App

In our affinity diagram for an app to help people get to class on time, we found our categories to be the design of the app, how the app helps users to navigate, what delays and other circumstances the app would notify you of, personal preference as to route and timing of arrival, and customization as to the user's needs of the app.

Team Personas: Possible End-Users

My individual persona is a made up person named Jake Seabrook. His quote is, "I want to work normally and have a good life in spite of my disability."

Team Storyboards: Navigational Situations

The storyboard I chose to make describes my persona, Jake, using the app to find a route to work with less hills and no stairs.

Team Sketches: Features and Screens

For our sketches, there would be a load up screen with the app's logo. Then, it would ask you for your preferences on what to avoid, like stairs or stoplights. Then, it would ask you to pick a destination with a search bar to find them. An informational would pop up with common obstructions, traffic patterns, etc. Then, you would be directed to the map, which has your destination, ETA, obstacles, and directions in a lower menu. There would also be an options screen that can be accessed by hitting a button after swiping up this lower menu. In this options menu, you can access and change your preferences and saved destinations.
